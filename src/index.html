Portraits of Gin

About

I have always liked gin. In a drink, absolutely, but almost even more on its own. Or with a pinch of vermouth - what we usually call a Dry Martini.
Early in 2020 I discovered that Sweden is a great producer of gin. We have well over 100 different sorts and brands at Systembolaget (the Swedish alcohol monopoly). And it's still increasing. So when corona hit us, the choice to go domestic was easy*.

NOTE! Some gins photographed are not domestic. Sorry for that.

As a member in a Facebook group devoted to gin, I started posting pictures of my Friday drink. Very soon the photos became almost as important as the drink itself. I would plan for next week's photo, and arranged the surroundings to fit the atmosphere. I started to include my gin-photos on my Instagram feed, and finally I made this website.
Even if the recipes are included, this is a website with pictures - or as I call them, portraits - of gin, more than a place for drinks themselves. ThatÂ´s why there are some pictures of water.
Well, on second thought I didn't want any gin to go to waste...

Now, enjoy the portraits and, if you want, the drinks.

Joacim Thenander, April 2021

Equipment

I started my "photo career" with Canon. First the cropped sensor cameras, then I moved on to full frame. But the last years I've worked mostly with Sony. I really like the fact that you can use any lens you want, with help of adapters.
One of my favourite lenses is the EF 50mm f/1,4 USM from Canon. I used it with my Canon cameras before, and now with the Sony. Although the Sony/Zeiss 55mm is the sharpest by far.
